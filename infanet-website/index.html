AI for Infant Development

Welcome to infaNet

Our mission is to collect infant images for future research that will support newborn development and growth, saving lives in the near and far future for the next generation.

By contributing to our dataset, you are helping us solve problems that humankind still doesn't know of. This dataset will train computer vision models to address known and unknown development issues worldwide.

How You Can Contribute

Contributing to infaNet is a wonderful way to support newborn development and growth through AI research. Here are a few ways you can get involved:

  • Submit Infant Images: If you have infant images that you can share, you can upload them through the "Contribute Now" button on the website. These images will be used to train computer vision models.
  • Spread the Word: Share information about infaNet with friends, family, and social media to help raise awareness and encourage more people to contribute.
  • Donate: Financial contributions can help support the research and development of the project. Look for a donation link or contact information on the website.
  • Volunteer: If you have skills in AI, data science, or healthcare, consider volunteering your time to help with the project.
  • Collaborate: If you are part of an organization or institution that can collaborate with infaNet, reach out to explore partnership opportunities.

Feel free to click the "Contribute Now" button on the website to get started or contact the project team for more details. Your support can make a significant impact on the future of infant health and development!

Photo Upload Guidelines

When submitting infant images, please ensure they meet the following quality guidelines and requirements:

Requirement Description
High resolution At least 1080p
Clear and well-lit Images should be clear and well-lit
Visible face Infant's face should be visible and unobstructed
Recent images Images should be recent (within the last 6 months)
Natural position Ensure the infant is in a natural and comfortable position

To submit your photos, click the "Contribute Now" button and attach the images to the email. Your contributions will help us build a robust dataset to train AI models for infant development research.
